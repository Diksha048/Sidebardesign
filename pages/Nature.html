Nature sites in Madhya Pradesh

1. Patalkot,Chhindwara


Patalkot (Sanskrit for "Very Deep") is a valley in the Tamia tehsil of Chhindwara district in Madhya Pradesh, India. Patalkot is 78 km away from Chhindwara district headquarter. This valley is home to a tribal culture and enormous herbal wealth.
  • In recent years, the government has been trying to make Patalkot an eco-tourism destination. The monsoon season is a popular time for visitors, as it is a sheltered area.
  • There are in total 12 villages and 13 hamlets with a total population of 2012 (1017 male and 995 female) in the valley. These villages are located at the distance of 2–3 km each.

2.Pachmarhi


The highest point in Madhya Pradesh, Pachmarhi is situated at an altitude of 1067 metres. This scenic town is a part of UNESCO Biosphere Reserve and is one of the most visited hill stations in Madhya Pradesh.
  • Location: Kandariya Mahadeo Temple, Khajuraho, Madhya Pradesh.

3. Amarkantak


Amarkantak is situated at an altitude of 1065 metres making it one of the highest hill stations in Madhya Pradesh. It has some of the most spectacular temples one will ever see
  • Location:Kranti Kriplani Nagar, Indore, Madhya Pradesh.

4.Marble Rocks of Bhedaghat


One of the most charming sites in the whole of Madhya Pradesh, Bhedaghat owes its fame to Marble Rocks that add the real grace to its landscape. It is a popular site for boating and kayaking and is frequented by locals and tourists alike. The factor that makes these 100 feet rocks – diverting the flow of the river Narmada – a natural wonder is how they appear to be changing shapes as you sail through them. The sight of these marble rocks glowing under a moonlit sky is truly magical and calling it wondrous would only justify its grandeur. You cannot miss this captivating site on your visit to Madhya Pradesh.
  • Location: Marble Rocks sit in the gushing stream of Narmada River at Bhedaghat, 25 km from Jabalpur.
